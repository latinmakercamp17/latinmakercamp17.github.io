Arduino!

The Arduino is is a small, programmable microcontroller board. A microcontroller is a self-contained computer that serves a given purpose - like the computer chips that run your microwave or control the engine in your car. You don't directly interact with microcontrollers like you do with a regular computer, but you use them all the time, probably without even knowing it.

We can program the chip on the Arduino board to send current to any one of the individual outputs - or "pins" on the board. By connecting a device such as an LED or a buzzer to a pin and supplying current to that pin, we can make light or noise.

The Arduino is also capable of reading current on some of the pins. By reading the current coming from a light or temperature sensor, an Arduino can act as a automatic nightlight or thermostat.

Today we will be looking at some of the basic principles behind circuit design and how we can use the Arduino to control those circuits!
